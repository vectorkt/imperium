The Imperium


A planar empire of countless worlds. Its creation was triggered when a group of sentients discovered the arcane arts in the lost depths of history. With magic for lifeblood, the Imperium and its miracles expanded endlessly throughout the planes, forever adding worlds. After all, the chances are higher of finding habitable worlds in a different plane than out in the cosmic void.


The many inhabitants of the Imperium stay in contact through a vast network of telepathic shards called the Shardscape. Naturally, the more powerful of mages began exploiting the Shardscape to backup their souls and downloading them into custom bodies, thus enabling them to transcend disease, death, and the limits of a single mortal shell.


Now that quasi-immortality had arrived the only resource that mattered was Mana, becoming the default imperial currency in its hardened form known as Tass. Such mages are known as Planars, especially when contrasted mortals.


The Rulers


No one truly knows who the ruler of the Imperium is. Although from time to time someone rises to claim the title of Emperor or Empress, sooner or later the Inevitable Army destroys the claimant. Perhaps the Inevitables are a royal guard, loyal only to the true ruler or rulers who lay hidden. Indeed, they often intervene when a threat from outside the far borders of the Imperium threatens the security of its citizens. Others think the Inevitables are a mechanism to prevent the rise of a tyrant. Critics point out this is probably not the case, as there are plenty of smaller tyrants in the many worlds. Some speculate the Army is the ruling body, while a select few think the Inevitables are a test to see who is fit for the throne.


Until a ruler actually manifests in one way or another, the Guilds hold sway. Headed by the mightiest of Planars, they often manage the day to day maintenance and development of the Imperium: maintaining the portals, mining Mana, obtaining rare raw resources, offering infrastructure and products to citizens. The greatest among the Guilds form the Consortium, legislating most of the business rules in the Imperium. While a Guild is not required to join the Consortium, membership brings great advantages, and no one dares rally against them. None, except for the Inevitables.


On magic


Day to day life in the Imperium varies greatly depending on what world one is. One thing is pervasive: the arcane arts. Knowledge of magic and access to Tass is what determines the social ladder; those at the absolute bottom have neither and serve their masters out of fear, while those at the top have both and have a lifestyle that would make kings envy. Indeed, the most adept of Planars are, for all practical purposes, gods.


Due to the high abundance of arcana it is hard to find someone in the Imperium who is not specialized into a field of magic. Indeed, it is rare to discover someone who has no arcane capabilities at all. Within the many worlds there are numerous schools of thought and practice on arcane; detailing them here would be pointless, as they range from divination to lactokinesis.


One thing arcanists have in common is the use of Mana. Mana is an energy equivalent to all things in nature, and follows conservation laws. The only difference from actual energy is that it has a pure form, a destructive, exothermic, mutative plasma; other forms can be found in nature, some still unstudied. One of these is the safe, solidified Tass, which is used as currency under the form of luminous coins.


There are various ways to obtain it. Some refine it from raw matter. Others generate it through living things or machines. Some sacrifice sentients, though some of the higher mages frown upon this and consider it barbaric. The best thing is to find a source of pure, primal Mana. It can turn up in the most unexpected places, and such hallows are fiercely guarded secrets. The ways of manipulating it are diverse as well: some channel it through supposed deities, others use complex rituals and symbols, while a few use it naturally, as a poet uses words to form verse. Indeed, many Planars combine various techniques.
